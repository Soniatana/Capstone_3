Capstone 3: West Region Sales Analysis with Power BI
Project Overview

This project was completed as part of the Year Up United Data Analytics Training Academy. The objective was to analyze four years of sales data (2022–2025) for the West Region of EmporiUm, a retailer that sells books, technology products, and educational merchandise through both online and in-store sales channels.

Using Power BI, I cleaned, modeled, analyzed, and visualized the data to identify sales trends, compare online and in-store performance, evaluate product category performance, and determine the region's top-selling books and authors. The report was designed to help management make informed, data-driven business decisions.

Objectives
Analyze West Region sales performance from 2022–2025.
Compare online and in-store sales.
Identify monthly sales trends.
Analyze sales performance by product category.
Identify top-selling books and corresponding authors.
Provide business recommendations based on the findings.

Executive summary
Key Findings
Online sales generated significantly more revenue than in-store sales.
Technology & Accessories was the highest-performing product category.
Monthly sales trends showed seasonal fluctuations, with several months consistently outperforming others.
Several authors and book titles consistently ranked among the top-selling products in both sales channels.
Interactive filters allow management to explore sales performance by year and product category.
Business Recommendations
Maintain sufficient inventory for best-selling books.
Continue investing in the online sales platform.
Promote high-performing authors and products through targeted marketing campaigns.
Improve lower-performing categories using promotions and bundled offers.
Prepare inventory before peak sales periods.
Continue using Power BI dashboards to support data-driven decision-making.
